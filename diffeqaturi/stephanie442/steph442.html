Systems of Difference Equations
Modeling the interaction of 2 species:

Lotka-Volterra Models

Predator Prey equations:
 
 
 

Nn+1= Nnf(Nn,Pn)
Pn+1= Nng(Nn,Pn)

 

such as

Nn+1=rNne-aPn
 Pn+1=Nn(1-e-aPn),

where a>0. This system has a positive equilibrium if and only if r > 1. In this case the positive equilibrium is unstable.  We are considering 2 interacting species without overlapping generations.  The growth of one population is affected by the growth of the other, whether the growth is positive or negative.  We also need to note http://www.stolaf.edu/people/mckelvey/envision.dir/nonDE.lotka-volt.html  this equation implies that the number of encounters a predator may have with a prey increases without bounds, but realistically, there is a limit to the predator's appetite.
 
 

Competing species such as
 

xn+1= xn/(a + yn)
yn+1= yn/(b + xn)

 where a, b > 0.
